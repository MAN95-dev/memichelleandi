The Two Dolphins

Overview

The Two Dolphins guesthouse is a charming accommodation option located in the picturesque town of Witsand, South Africa. This guesthouse is situated right on the banks of the beautiful Breede River, offering guests breathtaking views of the water and surrounding scenery.


I sat with key stakeholders to gather project requirements and understand their business. Then conducted market research to analyse competitors' offerings and identify potential opportunities. Based on these findings, built designs that met the needs and expectations of the stakeholders. This ensured that the project was aligned with business objectives and provided a user-friendly interface.

The Process

The Problem

One common problem when creating a guest house website is the challenge of effectively showcasing the unique features and amenities of the property, while also providing clear and easy-to-find information for potential guests. This can include issues such as limited visual assests, complex booking systems, mobile optimisation and standing out from the competition.

The Goal

To develop a website that is user-friendly and intuitive, with a clean and straightforward design that allows visitors to easily navigate and find the information they need. The aim is to create a simple yet effective platform that showcases all the features and amenities of the accommodation in a visually appealing manner, without overwhelming the user with unnecessary details. The priority is to ensure that the website provides a seamless and enjoyable user experience, allowing potential guests to make an informed decision about their stay.

Why?

Firstly, a website can help you reach a wider audience and attract more guests to your guest house. Many travelers use the internet to research and book accommodations, so having an online presence can increase your visibility and make it easier for potential guests to find you.

Secondly, a website can provide a convenient platform for guests to make reservations and inquiries. By including a booking form and contact information on your website, you can streamline the booking process and make it easier for guests to get in touch with you.

Thirdly, a website can help you showcase the unique features and amenities of your guest house. By including high-quality photos, descriptions, and reviews of your property, you can give potential guests a better idea of what to expect and why they should choose your guest house over others in the area.

Finally, a website can also help you establish your brand and build trust with potential guests. By including information about your history, values, and customer service policies, you can create a more personal connection with visitors and make them more likely to choose your guest house for their next vacation or getaway.

User Interviews

In the empathize stage, I conducted initial individual user interviews to understand what the users really wanted, and why. I chose a 6 questions, incorporating both qualitative and qunatitative aspects.

Style

Guerilla Interviews

Number

6 people interviewed

Duration

15 minutes each

Interview Type

Contextual Interviews

Initial Questions
  • What aspects of a guesthouse website are most important to you when planning a trip or searching for accommodation?

  • How do you usually find and book accommodations online? What websites or platforms do you prefer?

  • What features or functionalities do you expect to find on a modern guesthouse website that would enhance your booking experience?

  • How important is it for you to have clear and visually appealing images of the guesthouse's rooms, amenities, and surroundings on the website?

  • What types of additional information or content would you like to see on the website to get a better sense of the guesthouse's atmosphere and unique offerings?

  • What other elements or features do you think would set our guesthouse website apart from competitors and contribute to a positive online experience for potential guests?
